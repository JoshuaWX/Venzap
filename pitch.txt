2. DETAILED PROJECT IDEA AND PROBLEM STATEMENT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

THE PROBLEM — TWO SIDES, ONE CRISIS:

Nigeria has over 40 million micro and small businesses —
food vendors, grocery stores, pharmacies, laundry services,
fashion sellers — operating almost entirely offline and
invisible to the digital economy. Not because they lack 
customers, but because every existing platform excludes them.

Glovo and Chowdeck charge 20 to 30 percent commission per 
order, have complex onboarding processes that take weeks, 
and only serve established restaurants in major cities. 
Jumia and Konga require inventory systems built for large 
merchants. The average local vendor — the mama put, the 
campus pharmacy, the neighbourhood laundry — simply cannot 
access any of these platforms.

On the customer side, the problem is equally painful. 
Nigerians are resistant to downloading yet another delivery 
app for every vendor type they need. They are uncomfortable 
entering card details on unfamiliar platforms. And the idea 
of managing five different apps just to order food, buy 
medicine, and pick up laundry creates friction that pushes 
everything back to unstructured WhatsApp chats and cash 
transactions with zero records.

THE SOLUTION — VENZAP:

Venzap is a two-sided chat-commerce platform that brings 
any local vendor online in under ten minutes and lets 
customers order anything through Telegram — a platform 
millions of Nigerians already use daily — with zero app 
download required.

HOW IT WORKS:

For Vendors:
Vendors visit venzap.ng and register their business in 
minutes. They add their catalogue of products, set prices 
and a delivery fee, and go live immediately. From that 
moment, they receive incoming orders on a real-time 
dashboard with instant notifications. Payment is guaranteed 
and held in escrow before they fulfill any order. They 
never chase a customer for money again.

For Customers:
Customers open Telegram, find @VenzapBot, and create an 
account entirely inside the chat in under two minutes. 
The moment registration is complete, Venzap automatically 
provisions them a dedicated Nigerian bank account powered 
by Payaza's Dynamic Virtual Account API. Their wallet is 
funded simply by transferring money to that account number 
from any Nigerian bank app — exactly the way they send 
money to a friend. No payment links. No card details. No 
extra steps. Payaza detects the transfer, fires a webhook, 
and Venzap credits their wallet in real time with an 
instant Telegram notification.

From there, customers browse vendors and order in plain 
English or Nigerian Pidgin. Our hybrid AI system — powered 
by GPT-4o mini — understands "abeg I wan chop jollof from 
mama tunde" just as well as "I'd like Jollof Rice from 
Mama Tunde's Kitchen." The order reaches the vendor 
instantly. Payment is held in escrow and released 
automatically when the vendor confirms delivery.

LAUNCH STRATEGY — START FOCUSED, SCALE WIDE:

We are launching with food vendors because food has the 
highest order frequency and provides the fastest proof 
of concept. However, Venzap's entire infrastructure is 
designed from day one to support every vendor type. 
As the platform grows, we onboard grocery stores, 
pharmacies, laundry services, fashion vendors — any 
business that operates with a product catalogue.

Food is our entry point. Every local vendor in Nigeria 
is our market.

CHAT PLATFORM STRATEGY — TELEGRAM NOW, WHATSAPP NEXT:

We are currently building on Telegram because it provides 
a completely free, fully unrestricted bot API with zero 
business verification requirements and zero per-message 
fees. This allows us to build fast, deploy immediately, 
and prove the concept without cost barriers.

With funding, Venzap will expand to WhatsApp Business API 
— which has the largest user base in Nigeria — and 
subsequently to other widely adopted chat platforms. 
Venzap will become fully omnichannel. Telegram is the 
fastest route to market. WhatsApp is the scale play.

WHY VENZAP WINS:

Vendors go live in under ten minutes versus weeks on Glovo.
Zero commission model attracts vendor supply rapidly.
Customers need no new app — just Telegram they already have.
Every user gets a real Nigerian bank account for seamless 
wallet funding via plain bank transfer.
AI understands English and Nigerian Pidgin naturally.
Payaza powers every naira that moves on the platform.
Built to scale from food to every vendor category.
Built to scale from Telegram to WhatsApp and beyond.

3. HOW DOES YOUR PROJECT MEANINGFULLY INTEGRATE 
   ONE OR MORE PAYAZA APIs?
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Payaza is not an add-on to Venzap. Payaza is the financial 
engine the entire platform runs on. Remove Payaza and Venzap 
has no product. Here is every integration point in full:

INTEGRATION 1 — DYNAMIC VIRTUAL ACCOUNT API (PRIMARY INNOVATION)

This is the most significant Payaza integration and the 
financial foundation of Venzap's user experience.

The moment a user completes registration and verifies their 
email, Venzap automatically calls Payaza's Dynamic Virtual 
Account API to generate a real, dedicated Nigerian bank 
account number — for example, a Wema Bank account — assigned 
permanently to that user. The account name reads 
"John Doe - Venzap" so users always recognize it.

Venzap sends the account details directly to the user in 
Telegram immediately after registration. From that moment, 
to fund their Venzap wallet at any time, the user simply 
opens their GTB, Access, Opay, Kuda or any bank app and 
transfers money to their Venzap account number. That is it. 
No payment links. No card details. No extra apps.

Payaza detects the inbound transfer in real time, fires a 
webhook to our server, we verify the HMAC-SHA512 signature 
to confirm authenticity, and we credit the user's wallet 
atomically inside a database transaction with row-level 
locking. The user receives a Telegram notification within 
seconds: "✅ ₦5,000 received! Sender: GTB — JANE OSEI. 
New balance: ₦5,000."

This creates a wallet funding experience identical to 
sending money to a friend — the most trusted payment 
behaviour in Nigeria. No friction at all.

INTEGRATION 2 — PAYMENT LINK API (FALLBACK FUNDING)

For users who prefer not to do a direct bank transfer, 
Venzap generates a Payaza payment link on request and 
sends it directly inside the Telegram chat. Users can 
pay via card, bank transfer, or USSD on Payaza's 
hosted page. The same webhook handler detects the 
payment and credits the wallet automatically.

INTEGRATION 3 — ESCROW-BASED ORDER PAYMENT

When a customer confirms an order, Venzap does not send 
money to the vendor immediately. We debit the customer's 
Venzap wallet and hold the funds in escrow — tracked at 
the database level and backed by Payaza's infrastructure. 
The vendor sees the order, prepares and delivers. When 
they mark the order as delivered on their dashboard, 
the escrow is released automatically, the vendor's 
earnings update in real time, and a payout record is 
created in our system.

This protects both parties completely. Customers know 
their money is safe. Vendors know payment is guaranteed 
before they cook or prepare anything. Nobody can be cheated.

INTEGRATION 4 — WEBHOOK SECURITY INFRASTRUCTURE

Every Payaza webhook that hits our server goes through 
a strict security pipeline before any processing occurs. 
We read the raw request body before parsing, verify the 
HMAC-SHA512 signature using constant-time comparison to 
prevent timing attacks, and return HTTP 200 immediately 
to Payaza before any actual processing begins. We then 
check a webhook events log for idempotency to guarantee 
no payment is credited twice. All processing happens 
asynchronously via Celery background workers. Every 
webhook event is logged in full for auditability.

This is bank-grade webhook handling — not a demo.

WHY THIS INTEGRATION IS MEANINGFUL:

Most hackathon submissions add a payment button and call 
it "Payaza-powered." On Venzap, Payaza's Dynamic Virtual 
Account API is the core user experience. Payaza's webhook 
infrastructure is the real-time heartbeat of the platform. 
Payaza's payment link is the fallback. Payaza's financial 
rails are what make vendor payouts possible.

Venzap cannot function without Payaza. Every single feature 
— account creation, wallet funding, order payment, delivery 
confirmation, vendor earnings — depends on a Payaza 
integration. We believe this is exactly what meaningful 
integration looks like, and we believe it could serve as 
a flagship use case for what Payaza's DVA API can enable.

4. BUSINESS PLAN / MONETIZATION STRATEGY

Venzap operates a zero-commission freemium marketplace 
model — acquire vendors for free, monetise as they grow.

PHASE 1 — FREE LAUNCH (Months 1 to 3):
All vendors join free. Zero commission on orders. 
Goal is aggressive vendor acquisition across Lagos, 
Abuja and Ibadan to build supply density before 
activating revenue streams.

PHASE 2 — REVENUE ACTIVATION (Month 3 onwards):

REVENUE STREAM 1 — Vendor Subscription Tiers:

Starter (Free):
Up to 10 catalogue items, basic dashboard, 
email notifications only.

Pro — ₦5,000 per month:
Unlimited catalogue items, priority listing 
shown first to customers in their category, 
sales analytics showing top items and peak hours, 
real-time dashboard notifications, custom vendor 
profile page.

Business — ₦15,000 per month:
Everything in Pro plus multiple branch locations, 
staff and manager accounts, monthly revenue reports, 
and dedicated customer support.

REVENUE STREAM 2 — Platform Transaction Fee:
1.5 percent on every order processed through Venzap.
Small enough that vendors accept it without friction.
Scales directly and automatically with order volume.

At 1,000 daily orders averaging ₦3,500 each, 
daily GMV is ₦3.5 million. The 1.5 percent fee 
produces ₦52,500 per day — ₦1.575 million per month 
from transaction fees alone, before any subscriptions.

REVENUE STREAM 3 — Promoted Listings:
Vendors pay ₦10,000 to ₦50,000 per month to appear 
at the top of their category in their city. Works 
exactly like search advertising — high margin, 
zero operational cost for Venzap.

REVENUE STREAM 4 — Expansion Revenue:
As we onboard grocery stores, pharmacies, laundry 
services and fashion vendors, each new category 
opens a new subscriber base using the exact same 
infrastructure already built. Revenue scales without 
proportional cost increase.

THE CORE FINANCIAL ARGUMENT FOR VENDOR ADOPTION:

A vendor doing ₦1,000,000 per month in sales pays 
Glovo between ₦200,000 and ₦300,000 in commission.
The same vendor on Venzap pays ₦15,000 in transaction 
fees and ₦5,000 in Pro subscription — ₦20,000 total.
We are ten to fifteen times cheaper than Glovo.
That is not a feature. That is the entire vendor 
acquisition pitch, and it sells itself.

Monthly server and operational costs: approximately ₦350,000.
Break-even point: 70 Pro subscribers (subscriptions alone)

Conservative Month 6 projection:

Payaza's Dynamic Virtual Account API behaviour under high 
concurrency and edge case webhook scenarios has not been 
fully stress-tested by our team at this stage. Telegram 
bot performance at high concurrent user load also requires 
production monitoring. These are known, manageable risks 
that we plan to address immediately post-hackathon with 
proper infrastructure investment.

WHY CONFIDENTLY 6 — ZERO EXPERIMENTAL TECHNOLOGY:

Every single component in our stack is battle-tested 
and used in production globally:

FastAPI powers real fintech APIs in production worldwide.
PostgreSQL is the world's most reliable open-source database.
python-telegram-bot is a mature, well-documented library 
with thousands of production deployments.
Redis is the industry standard for session state and caching.
Next.js is used by Vercel, TikTok, Twitch and major companies.
GPT-4o mini is stable, officially documented and affordable.
Payaza has a documented API with webhook support.
Celery with Redis is the standard async task pattern in Python.

EVERY FAILURE POINT HAS A BUILT-IN FALLBACK:

If the LLM fails or times out, the rule-based engine takes 
over silently and the user sees a clean menu of options.
If the AI token budget is exceeded, the system continues 
running in full rule-based mode — users never notice.
If a DVA webhook is delayed, the transaction is queued 
in Celery and retried automatically up to three times.
If the vendor dashboard is offline, the vendor receives 
an instant email notification for every new order.

The product works completely and smoothly even if every 
advanced feature fails. That is deliberate engineering, 
not luck. That is the difference between a demo that 
crashes on stage and one that wins.
